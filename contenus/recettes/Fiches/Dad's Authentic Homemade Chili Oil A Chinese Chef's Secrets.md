## Ingrédients

- sichuan dried piment flakes
- gingembre
- 1 oz red oignon
- 1 oz shallot
- 3 clove ail
- 2 piece star anise
- 1 dash graines de sésame
- 4 oz neutral oil
- 1 tsp huile de sésame
- sel
- sucre
- white poivre
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1 tbsp neutral oil

## Instructions

- [ ] First, we'll create a flavorful chili mix as the seasoning base for the chili oil. In a medium, heat-safe bowl, mix together chili flakes, salt, sugar, and white pepper.
- [ ] Cut the ginger and red onion into slices. They'll be frying in hot oil, so don't fuss over getting them too paper-thin.
- [ ] Heat up your wok over high heat for about 30 seconds, and pour the oil in.
- [ ] When the aromatic oil has come back up to 300°F (149°C), turn off the heat; it's time for the (somewhat) dramatic pour-over. Don't pour it all at once! Dumping a huge volume of hot oil at once will scorch and burn the chili mix.
